<p><p>PM2.5 sensors are devices that measure the concentration of fine particulate matter in the air. There are two main types of PM2.5 sensors: Infrared sensors and laser sensors. Infrared sensors work by detecting particles using their absorption of infrared light, while laser sensors use a laser beam to detect particles based on light scattering. Both types of sensors are commonly used in air quality monitoring systems to provide real-time data on the levels of PM2.5 pollutants in the atmosphere.</p></p>
