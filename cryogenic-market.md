<p><p>Cryogenic market consists of two main types: storage and distribution. Storage refers to the process of keeping cryogenic materials, such as liquefied gases and frozen biological samples, in specialized containers or tanks at extremely low temperatures. This ensures their preservation and availability for various applications like medical research or food preservation. On the other hand, distribution involves the transportation and delivery of cryogenic products from storage facilities to end-users, such as hospitals or industrial sectors, while maintaining their cold temperature conditions. These market types are critical components of the cryogenic industry, ensuring the efficient handling and utilization of cryogenic materials.</p></p>
