One of the key drivers of this market is the increasing need to upgrade aging elevator systems in commercial, residential, and industrial buildings. As elevator technology advances rapidly, older lifts become outdated and less efficient. Hence, building owners and facility managers are increasingly inclined towards lift modernisation to improve reliability and reduce maintenance costs.</p><p>The trend of smart cities and sustainable infrastructure also contributes to the growth of the lift modernisation market. Governments worldwide are focusing on developing smart cities that are interconnected and energy-efficient. Upgrading elevators with advanced control and monitoring systems allow for better management, increased safety, and reduced energy consumption.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing emphasis on elevator safety regulations and compliance with standards set by regulatory bodies drives the demand for lift modernisation services. <p><ul><li>Hydraulic</li><li>Traction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Lift modernisation market types are categorised into hydraulic and traction systems. Hydraulic lifts use fluid pressure to move the elevator car, making them suitable for low-rise buildings and home installations. Traction lifts, on the other hand, employ steel cables and a motor-driven pulley system to lift the car, making them preferable for high-rise buildings. Both systems aim to improve the efficiency, safety, and performance of existing lifts and cater to different types of structures, needs, and budgets.</p></p>
