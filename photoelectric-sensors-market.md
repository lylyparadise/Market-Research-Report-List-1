<p><p>Photoelectric sensors are devices used to detect the presence or absence of an object by measuring the amount of reflected or transmitted light. There are three main types of photoelectric sensors in the market. Reflective photoelectric sensors work by emitting and detecting light reflected by the object. Diffuse photoelectric sensors emit and receive light from the same housing, detecting objects by measuring the intensity of reflected light. Through-beam photoelectric sensors consist of separate emitter and receiver units, where an object is detected when it interrupts the beam between them.</p></p>
