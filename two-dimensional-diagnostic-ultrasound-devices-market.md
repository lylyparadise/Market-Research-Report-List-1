Fanshaped scanning is a type that uses a probe with a wide-angle beam and produces a fan-shaped image. Linear scanning uses a probe with a narrow beam and produces a linear or rectangular image. Convex array scanning uses a probe with a curved beam and produces a convex image. These different scanning types allow physicians to capture images from different angles and perspectives, aiding in the accurate diagnosis of various medical conditions.</p></p>
