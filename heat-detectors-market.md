<p><p>Heat detectors are devices used to identify the presence of fire or excessive heat. There are various types available in the market. Fixed temperature detectors are activated when a specific temperature threshold is reached. Differential temperature detectors, on the other hand, are triggered when a significant difference in temperature is detected within a short period. Poor fixed temperature detectors are less accurate and have a higher margin of error. Additionally, there are other types of heat detectors that incorporate advanced technologies like rate-of-rise detectors and combination detectors, offering additional features and functionalities for fire detection and prevention.</p></p>
