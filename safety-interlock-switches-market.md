<p><p>Safety interlock switches are devices used to ensure the safety of machines and equipment by preventing unauthorized access or operation. Two main types of safety interlock switches are noncontact safety interlock switches and mechanical safety interlock switches. Noncontact safety interlock switches use proximity or magnetic sensing technologies to detect when an object is within a certain distance, while mechanical safety interlock switches use physical mechanisms such as keys or levers to control access. Both types serve the same purpose of protecting workers and preventing accidents, but differ in their sensing mechanisms.</p></p>
