<p><p>Continuous blood glucose monitoring (CGM) is a technique that measures glucose levels in real-time throughout the day and night. It involves the use of a small sensor that is placed under the skin to measure glucose levels in the interstitial fluid. The sensor is connected to a transmitter that sends the data to a receiver or smartphone, where the glucose levels can be monitored and analyzed.</p><p>The CGM market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to the increasing prevalence of diabetes, rising awareness about the benefits of CGM devices, and technological advancements in sensor technology. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.90% during the forecast period.</p><p>One of the major drivers of market growth is the increasing prevalence of diabetes worldwide. CGM devices are becoming more user-friendly and convenient, making it easier for individuals with diabetes to monitor their glucose levels on a regular basis. The integration of CGM devices with smartphones and other digital platforms also allows for easier data management and analysis.</p><p>Technological advancements in CGM devices, such as improved accuracy, longer sensor wear duration, and the development of closed-loop systems, are also driving market growth. <p><p>Continuous blood glucose monitoring (CGM) market encompasses two types: self-monitoring blood glucose systems (SMBG) and continuous glucose monitoring systems (CGMS). SMBG allows individuals to measure their blood glucose levels using portable devices like glucose meters. On the other hand, CGMS provides real-time glucose measurements through a sensor implanted under the skin, eliminating the need for fingerstick tests. Both systems aim to provide accurate and constant glucose monitoring, empowering individuals with diabetes to monitor their blood glucose levels effectively. The CGM market caters to the diverse needs of individuals seeking convenient and reliable glucose monitoring solutions.</p></p>
