Additionally, the rising adoption of minimally invasive techniques over traditional open surgeries is boosting the market's growth.</p><p>Moreover, there are several trends observed in the interventional neurology devices market. One significant trend is the increasing use of robotic-assisted devices in neurovascular interventions. Robotic systems provide enhanced precision and control during procedures, leading to improved patient outcomes. Another trend is the development of bioresorbable devices that are designed to dissolve over time, eliminating the need for additional intervention to remove permanent implants. These devices offer advantages such as reduced risk of infection, enhanced healing process, and improved patient comfort.</p><p>Overall, the interventional neurology devices market is poised for growth due to the increasing prevalence of neurovascular diseases, advancements in technology, and the shift towards minimally invasive procedures. <p><ul><li>Aneurysm Coiling and Embolization Devices</li><li>Angioplasty Devices</li><li>Micro Support Devices</li><li>Neurothrombectomy Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Interventional neurology devices market consists of various types of devices used in the diagnosis and treatment of neurological disorders. Aneurysm coiling and embolization devices are used to treat brain aneurysms by diverting blood flow away from the aneurysm. Angioplasty devices are used to widen narrowed blood vessels in the brain. Micro support devices provide support during surgeries and help in the navigation of catheters. Neurothrombectomy devices are used to remove blood clots from blood vessels in the brain, preventing strokes. These devices play a crucial role in improving patient outcomes and quality of life.</p></p>
