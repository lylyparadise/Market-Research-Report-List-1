Several factors are contributing to this market growth. Firstly, the increasing number of surgical procedures, including orthopedic, cardiovascular, and general surgeries, is driving the demand for surgical drains and wound drainage systems. Additionally, the growing prevalence of chronic wounds, such as diabetic ulcers and pressure sores, is further propelling the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in the design of surgical drains and wound drainage systems are also fueling market expansion. These advancements include the development of disposable and self-suctioning drains, which provide improved safety and convenience for patients and healthcare professionals. Furthermore, the rising adoption of minimally invasive surgeries and the growing focus on reducing hospital stays are expected to drive the demand for surgical drains and wound drainage devices globally.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards the adoption of closed suction drainage systems over open suction drainage systems. Closed suction systems offer several advantages, such as reduced risk of infection, enhanced patient comfort, and increased efficiency in fluid removal. Furthermore, the increasing preference for disposable drains over reusable drains due to concerns over infection control and cross-contamination is another emerging trend in the market.</p><p>Overall, the global surgical drains and wound drainage market is poised for substantial growth due to the increasing number of surgeries, the rising prevalence of chronic wounds, and advancements in drainage system technologies.</p></p>

<p><p>Surgical drains are medical devices used to remove excess fluid or blood from a surgical site. Passive drains are simpler in design and less expensive, but may require frequent emptying. Active drains offer enhanced drainage and reduce the risk of blockage.
