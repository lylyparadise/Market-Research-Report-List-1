<p><p>An intraosseous device is a medical tool used to access the bone marrow for the administration of fluids, medications, and blood products directly into the circulation when intravenous access is difficult or impossible. It is a minimally invasive procedure that involves the insertion of a needle or catheter into the bone marrow cavity, usually in the long bones such as the femur or tibia.</p><p>The intraosseous device market is experiencing significant growth due to its advantages over traditional methods of gaining vascular access. These devices provide a reliable and rapid alternative when the intravenous route is not feasible, such as during emergencies, in pediatric patients, or in patients with difficult vascular access. The market growth is also attributed to the increasing incidences of chronic diseases, trauma cases, and a rising geriatric population.</p><p>Additionally, the market is being driven by the advancements in technology that have led to the development of technologically advanced intraosseous devices, such as automatic intraosseous devices and drill-powered devices, which offer improved ease of use and patient comfort.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has further boosted the growth of the intraosseous device market as healthcare professionals increasingly rely on these devices for the rapid administration of medications and fluids to critically ill patients.</p><p>The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.6% during the forecast period.
