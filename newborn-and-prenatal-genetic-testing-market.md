<p><p>Newborn and prenatal genetic testing utilize various methods to identify genetic disorders and abnormalities in unborn babies or newborns. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR), Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH), Array Comparative Genomic Hybridization (aCGH), Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing (NIPT), and Maternal Serum Screening (MSS) are some common types of tests. PCR amplifies DNA to detect specific genetic sequences, FISH uses fluorescent probes to identify abnormalities, aCGH examines the entire genome for copy number variations, NIPT detects fetal DNA in maternal blood, and MSS measures certain proteins to assess the risk of chromosomal disorders.</p></p>
