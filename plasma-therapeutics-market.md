<p><h1>Plasma Therapeutics Market Size: Evaluating its Market Trends, Growth, and Projections 2024 - 2031</h1></p><p><strong>Plasma Therapeutics Market Analysis and Latest Trends</strong></p>
<p><p>Plasma therapeutics, also known as blood-derived products or blood fractionation products, refer to the therapeutic use of plasma-derived biological products for various medical conditions. These products are prepared by fractionating human plasma to isolate and extract specific proteins, immunoglobulins, coagulation factors, and other blood components. Plasma therapeutics are commonly used in the treatment of immune deficiencies, bleeding disorders, autoimmune diseases, and neurological disorders, among others.</p><p>According to market research, the global plasma therapeutics market is projected to witness a significant growth rate during the forecast period. This growth is attributed to several factors, including the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, growing geriatric population, rising demand for plasma-derived products, and advancements in plasma collection technologies.</p><p>Furthermore, the market growth is also driven by the increasing adoption of plasma therapeutics in new indications, such as the treatment of respiratory diseases and COVID-19. The COVID-19 pandemic has led to a surge in demand for convalescent plasma therapy, which involves the use of plasma from recovered COVID-19 patients for treating infected individuals. This has further boosted the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements, such as the development of nanofiltration techniques for virus removal during the production of plasma-derived products, are also expected to contribute to market expansion. Additionally, the growing awareness and acceptance of plasma-derived therapies in emerging markets, along with the expanding applications in regenerative medicine and tissue engineering, are anticipated to provide lucrative opportunities for market players.</p><p>In conclusion, the plasma therapeutics market is poised to experience significant growth during the forecast period, with a projected CAGR of 11.1%. Factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, advancements in collection technologies, expanding applications, and the surge in demand due to the COVID-19 pandemic are expected to drive market growth.</p></p>
