<p><p>Cloud servers can be categorized into four main types based on their deployment models: Public Cloud, Private Cloud, Hybrid Cloud, and Community Cloud. </p><p>Public Cloud servers are owned and operated by third-party service providers, offering services to multiple users over the internet. Private Cloud servers are exclusively used by a single organization, providing more control and security. Hybrid Cloud servers combine both public and private cloud models, allowing organizations to move workloads between them as needed. Community Cloud servers are shared by several organizations with common interests, such as security and compliance requirements.</p></p>
