<p><p>The Bone Screw System Market consists of several types of screws used in orthopedic procedures. Locking screws provide more stability as they lock into the plate or implant, preventing movement. Cannulated screws have a hollow core that allows for the insertion of a guide wire. Conventional screws are typical screws used in bone fixation. Headless screws have no protruding head, making them less visible after implantation. These different types cater to different surgical needs and offer various benefits in terms of stability, precision, and aesthetics.</p></p>
