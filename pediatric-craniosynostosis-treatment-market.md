<p><p>Pediatric craniosynostosis refers to the condition where one or more of an infant's cranial sutures close too early, leading to abnormal skull growth. The treatment for pediatric craniosynostosis typically involves surgery to correct the abnormal skull shape and allow for the growth and development of the brain. The main goal of the surgery is to relieve the pressure on the brain and create a more normal-looking head shape.</p><p>The surgical procedures for pediatric craniosynostosis treatment include endoscopic strip craniectomy, open cranial vault reshaping, and minimally invasive techniques. The choice of procedure depends on the severity of the condition, age of the child, and other factors assessed by the healthcare professional.</p><p>The Pediatric Craniosynostosis Treatment Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
