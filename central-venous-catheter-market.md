<p><p>A Central Venous Catheter (CVC) is a thin, flexible tube that is inserted into a large vein in the body, typically the superior vena cava, near the heart. The market growth can be attributed to various factors such as the rising prevalence of chronic diseases, increasing surgical procedures, technological advancements in catheter design, and the growing geriatric population.</p><p>The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and kidney diseases, is driving the demand for central venous catheters. These catheters provide an efficient way of delivering medications and nutrients directly into the bloodstream, ensuring faster and more effective treatment. Moreover, the increasing number of surgical procedures, particularly in the field of oncology and intensive care, is also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Technological advancements in central venous catheter design, such as antimicrobial-coated catheters and improved insertion techniques, are also driving market growth. These advancements aim to reduce the risk of catheter-associated bloodstream infections and improve patient comfort and safety.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing geriatric population worldwide is fueling the demand for central venous catheters. Elderly individuals often require long-term medication administration or nutritional support, making central venous catheters a preferred choice.</p><p>Overall, with the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, growing surgical procedures, technological advancements, and the aging population, the Central Venous Catheter Market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 12.60% during the forecast period.</p></p>

Single-lumen catheters consist of a single channel for fluid administration or blood withdrawal. Double-lumen catheters have two separate channels, allowing for simultaneous infusion and blood sampling. Triple-lumen catheters have three channels, enabling multiple infusions or medications to be administered together.
