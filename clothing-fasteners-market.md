<p><p>Clothing fasteners refer to various types of devices that secure garments or fabrics together. Zippers, commonly used on garments such as pants and jackets, consist of interlocking metal or plastic teeth that can be opened or closed by a sliding mechanism. Buttons, typically made of plastic or metal, are attached to garments through stitching and provide a secure closure. Snaps, also known as press studs, involve two interlocking discs that can be fastened or opened with pressure. Other clothing fasteners include hooks, clasps, buckles, and Velcro, offering different mechanisms to secure clothing in place.</p></p>
