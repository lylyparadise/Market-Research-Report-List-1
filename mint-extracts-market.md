<p><p>Mint extracts are available in two main market types: liquid and powder. Liquid mint extracts are made by macerating mint leaves in alcohol or water, resulting in a concentrated liquid form that preserves the flavor and aroma of mint. These extracts are commonly used in baking, cooking, and various beverage applications. On the other hand, mint extracts in powder form are created by dehydrating the liquid extract, resulting in a powdered form that is easier to store and has a longer shelf life. Both liquid and powder mint extracts offer the same minty taste and can be used interchangeably in recipes.</p></p>
