<p><p>Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC) is a technique used in liquid chromatography to separate molecules based on their size. It relies on a porous stationary phase that allows smaller molecules to enter the pores, resulting in longer retention times, while larger molecules elute faster due to their exclusion from the pores. SEC is commonly used in the analysis of biomolecules, such as proteins, peptides, and nucleic acids.</p><p>The Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC) HPLC column is an essential component in SEC analysis. This column is designed with a stationary phase that consists of interconnected and uniform pores to accurately separate molecules based on their size. The size exclusion media within the column can vary, depending on the specific application requirements.</p><p>The Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC) HPLC Column Market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors.
