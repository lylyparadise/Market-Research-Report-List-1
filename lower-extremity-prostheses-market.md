<p><p>The Lower Extremity Prostheses Market includes various types of artificial limbs to replace lower limb amputations. Above Knee Prosthesis is designed for amputations above the knee, providing support and mobility. Below-knee Prosthesis is used for amputations below the knee, enabling walking and weight-bearing. Ankle Prosthesis replaces a missing ankle joint, aiding in movement and stability. Lastly, Hip Disarticulation Prosthesis is for amputations at the hip joint, allowing amputees to perform activities like walking and running. These prosthetic devices enhance the quality of life for individuals with lower limb amputations.</p></p>
