<p><p>Multi-channel audio codecs refer to technologies used to compress and decompress audio signals in various formats. Analog codecs deal with the transmission and storage of audio signals in their original analog form. They are commonly used in older systems and have limitations in terms of quality and efficiency. Digital codecs, on the other hand, convert audio signals into digital data before transmission or storage. They offer higher quality, enhanced efficiency, and support for various audio formats, making them the preferred choice in modern audio systems.</p></p>
