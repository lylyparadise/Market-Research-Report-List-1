<p><p>Beverage flavors can be categorized into two types - natural and artificial. Natural flavors are derived from real food sources such as fruits, vegetables, herbs, and spices. They are created using processes like extraction, distillation, or fermentation. Artificial flavors, on the other hand, are chemically synthesized substances that imitate natural flavors. They are typically cheaper to produce and offer greater consistency in taste. While both types are widely used in the beverage industry, the choice between natural and artificial flavors may depend on factors like cost, availability, and consumer preferences.</p></p>
