<p><p>A Michelson Interferometer is an optical instrument that uses the principles of interference to measure the wavelength of light and make precise measurements of length. It was invented by Albert Abraham Michelson in the late 19th century and has since become an essential tool in many fields, including physics, astronomy, and engineering.</p><p>The interferometer works by splitting a beam of light into two separate paths, reflecting them back towards each other, and then recombining them. The resulting interference pattern can be measured and analyzed to determine the properties of the light source or the length of an object being measured.</p><p>In terms of market growth analysis, the Michelson Interferometer market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
