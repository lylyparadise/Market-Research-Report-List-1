<p><p>The handheld pyranometer market consists of two types: thermopile and photovoltaic. Thermopile pyranometers measure solar radiation by utilizing multiple thermocouples, which generate an electric current proportional to the temperature difference. On the other hand, photovoltaic pyranometers use silicon photovoltaic cells to convert solar radiation into direct current electricity. Both types are portable and designed for handheld use, providing measurements of solar radiation intensity. The thermopile pyranometer market is characterized by its reliance on thermoelectric principles, whereas the photovoltaic market utilizes photovoltaic technology for sensing solar radiation.</p></p>
